What is the importance of due diligence?

Due Diligence is the process of investigating any potential risks that might be associated with your business, before making any transaction, whether it is for acquisition, merge, sale or refinancing; to avoid potential risks, and maximize value in a transaction through understanding, and assessing all of the legal issues.

What are the types of taxes?

There are two main types of taxes:

  • Company profit taxes:

The corporate tax is imposed on companies’ operating earnings, after deducting expenses form its revenues. The taxable net profit is determined based upon the auditor financial reports after adjusting it based on the income tax law.

  • Individual income taxes:

Individual income taxes is imposed on the total net income of the resident individuals for income earned in Egypt, as well as the income earned outside Egypt for resident individuals whose center of commercial, industrial, or professional activities is in Egypt.

Salary Tax: Under the Egyptian Tax Law, it is the employer responsibility to withhold the salary tax due on each employee and remit the tax to the Tax Authority on a monthly basis.

Why it is important for business entities operating in Egypt
to register for social insurance?

Business entities operating in Egypt should register for social insurance to legalize its situation before the competent authorities.


The social insurance authority will also have access to the tax files of the employers in order to obtain correct information in relation to employees’ salaries. Such measures will be adopted by the social insurance authority to prevent employers’ evasion of their obligations under the social insurance Law. (Law no. 148 year 2019)

What is the difference between an One person company and
a private enterprise?

The One person company is a new type of companies that was introduced in the company’s law No. 159 of 1981 after its latest amendments –

(Law no. 4 year 2018). The company is owned by one juristic or natural person, the liability of the investor is limited to the value of the (shared) capital.

Private Enterprise is a legal establishment that is owned by one person that is considered a merchant. The liability of the owner is not limited to the capital of this establishment but shall extend to include his private assets.

What is an electronic invoice?

As a part of Egypt’s digital transformation article 35 of law number 206 of 2020 stated that companies and other legal and natural persons who sell a commodity or provide a service must register all their purchases and sales of goods and services on the electronic system.

E-invoice is defined as an electronic document which proves the commercial transactions concluded between persons, for the sale of goods and services. Such document has to meet certain requirements and should be signed electronically.
